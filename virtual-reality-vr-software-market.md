<p><p>The Virtual Reality (VR) software market can be categorized into two primary types: on-premise and cloud-based solutions. On-premise VR software is installed locally on a user's hardware, offering higher customization, control, and low latency, making it suitable for industries requiring intensive processing, such as gaming and medical training. In contrast, cloud-based VR software leverages remote servers, enabling easy access, scalability, and lower upfront costs, making it ideal for collaborative environments and users with limited hardware capabilities.</p></p>
